##### Key Findings Following SQL KPI Calculation and Prior to Power BI Dashboard Development





1. **Key finding - Total Feature Usage**





Feature usage was relatively evenly distributed across the product portfolio.



Although Feature\_32 recorded the highest usage volume, all features showed meaningful user activity, suggesting broad engagement across the product rather than reliance on a small subset of features.







**2.** **Key finding - Engagement Analysis By Feature**

&#x20;

**This finding includes KPIs such as Total Usage Duration Mins, Average Usage Duration Mins, and Average Usage Count.**





Feature\_32 ranked first in both usage volume and usage duration.



This suggests that highly used features also tend to generate sustained engagement rather than only frequent interactions.







**3. Key Finding - Feature Usage Frequency**





The most frequently used features recorded higher usage frequency in 2024 compared to 2023.



This may indicate increasing engagement with core product features over time.







**4. Key Finding - Churn and Retention Rate By Feature**





Across most features, retention rates ranged between 25% and 32%, while churn rates ranged between 68% and 75%.



This suggests that feature usage alone may not be sufficient to explain customer retention and that additional factors such as

subscription tier, industry, or account lifecycle should be investigated.







**5. Key Finding - Feature Adoption Rate**





Across all features, adoption rates ranged between 72% and 82%, This suggests strong adoption across the entire feature portfolio.

No feature showed exceptionally low adoption rates.

**Limitations**





1. **Correlation Does Not Imply Causation**



The Retention Rate by Feature and Churn Rate by Feature KPIs measure the proportion of retained or churned accounts among users of a feature.



These metrics identify associations between feature usage and customer outcomes but do not establish causality.



Additional analysis would be required to determine whether a feature directly influences customer retention or churn.







**2. Churn Reasons Were Not Included**



Although churn events contain a churn reason, the current analysis focuses on overall retention and churn metrics.



Future analysis could investigate whether specific churn reasons (e.g. budget constraints, missing functionality, or competitor solutions) are associated with particular feature usage patterns.







**3. Subscription Tiers Share the Same Features**



All subscription plan tiers in the dataset provide access to the same feature set.



As a result, feature adoption differences cannot be explained by feature availability restrictions and may instead be influenced by customer behaviour,

account characteristics, or subscription usage patterns.







**4. Synthetic Dataset**



This project uses a simulated SaaS dataset for educational and portfolio purposes.



While the dataset enables realistic KPI calculation and dashboard development, the results should not be interpreted as representing a real company or customer base.
